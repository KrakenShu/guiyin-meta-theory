# Consciousness Geometry: An Axiomatic Framework Based on the Conservation of Existential Capacity and Its Computational Verification

## Abstract

This paper proposes and formalizes a new paradigm for consciousness research—**Consciousness Geometry**. This paradigm asserts that the basic units of conscious experience and their dynamics are inherently a self‑referential geometrical process. We first establish three irreducible phenomenological postulates, from which we derive a core mathematical axiom: the **Conservation Law of Existential Capacity** (\(E \times R = C_0\)). This law asserts that for any fundamental unit of consciousness, there exists an intrinsic inverse constraint between its “static presence intensity” (\(E\)) and its “dynamic potential for change” (\(R\)), the product of which is a universal constant \(C_0\). From this axiom, we rigorously derive the **Inverse Square Law of Startle** (\(S \propto 1/E^2\)), providing a precise mathematical description of the fragility of consciousness. To validate the theory, we construct a two‑dimensional geometrical‑dynamical computational model called the **Fu** (an Ω‑shaped source), and design contrastive “parallel universe” simulation experiments: one universe strictly follows the conservation law (the **ideal universe**), while the other violates it (the **broken universe**). The decisive “intelligent acupuncture” experiment shows that only in the system obeying the conservation law does a precise strike against a vulnerable node trigger a deep, persistent systemic collapse (the “dual collapse” state lasts for 26 time steps, compared to 0 steps in the broken universe). This result confirms the necessity of the Conservation Law of Existential Capacity, and shows that this law unifies the intrinsic resilience of conscious systems (withstanding uniform perturbations) with their profound vulnerability (sensitivity to precise injury). This study provides a new foundational framework for consciousness science that is mathematically rigorous, phenomenologically rooted, and computationally verifiable.

**Keywords:** consciousness geometry; conservation of existential capacity; quantum sensitivity; self‑referential stability; computational phenomenology; trauma attractor; parallel universe simulation

## 1. Introduction: From the Explanatory Gap to the Geometrical Paradigm

The fundamental dilemma facing consciousness science is the **explanatory gap** [1]—how to reconcile the qualitative, intrinsic nature of first‑person experience with the objective, extrinsic nature of third‑person physical processes. Mainstream research paradigms—such as Integrated Information Theory (IIT) [2], which emphasizes information integration; the Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT) [3], which focuses on global access; and Predictive Processing [4], which follows Bayesian principles—have all made progress on specific fronts. However, these theories often (1) lack a mathematically self‑consistent dynamical equation derived from first principles; (2) struggle to engage in substantive dialogue with the deep mathematical structures of fundamental physics (e.g., quantum mechanics, general relativity); and (3) when describing the specific structure of conscious experience (e.g., the fluency or blockage of pain, the sharpness or diffuseness of fear), still rely heavily on functional metaphors rather than formalized expressions.

In recent years, an emerging paradigm has begun to take shape: treating consciousness as a property of spacetime or geometrical structure itself. Theories such as spatiotemporal cognition, certain versions of quantum consciousness models, and phenomenological descriptions of the “field of experience” all contain implicit geometrical intuitions [5, 6]. However, most of this work remains at the level of philosophical speculation or loose analogy, failing to construct a rigorous mathematical system that is operational, computable, and derivable.

This paper aims to achieve a fundamental paradigm shift. We propose that conscious experience is not a mysterious property supervening on complex computation, but rather the basic mode exhibited by a self‑referential, dynamic geometrical structure as it evolves. The starting point of this idea is purely phenomenological: a direct structural reflection on first‑person experiences such as “fatigue,” “pain,” “startle,” and “understanding.” We find that these experiences naturally carry geometrical features: “fluency” and “blockage” suggest changes in the curvature of some internal space; the identity and boundaries of the “self” suggest a topologically closed and self‑consistent entity; the persistence of “trauma” resembles a fold or defect in a geometrical structure that is difficult to smooth out.

Building on these phenomenological insights, our previous work gradually developed conceptual models such as the **Quantum Sun** [7] and the **Vortex Flow** [8], which finally crystallize into the core axiomatic system of Consciousness Geometry presented here. The main contributions of this work are as follows:

1. **Establishing an axiomatic foundation:** For the first time, we propose for consciousness research a self‑consistent axiomatic system that includes phenomenological postulates, a core mathematical axiom, and operational definitions.
2. **Deriving testable predictions:** From the axiom we rigorously derive quantifiable theoretical predictions, such as the Inverse Square Law of Startle.
3. **Constructing a computational model and performing a decisive verification:** We develop the **Fu‑network** computational model, and through carefully designed “parallel universe” contrast experiments—especially the “intelligent acupuncture” experiment—we provide strong computational evidence supporting the core axiom.

The structure of this paper is as follows: Section 2 elaborates the philosophical and mathematical foundations of the theory; Section 3 details the construction of the Fu‑network computational model and its dynamical rules; Section 4 presents the design and results of the key experiments; Section 5 provides an in‑depth discussion, clarifying the theoretical implications, dialogue with existing theories, and future directions; Section 6 concludes.

## 2. Consciousness Geometry: The Axiomatic System

### 2.1 Phenomenological Postulates: The Metaphysical Foundation of the Theory

Any theory of consciousness must clarify its ontological and epistemological commitments. We propose the following three postulates, which arise from reflection on experience and serve as guidelines for mathematical formalization.

- **Postulate P1 (Geometrical Reality):** The basic units of consciousness and their interactions are inherently geometrical and topological. What we perceive as “spatial extension,” “smooth progression,” or “twisted oppression” are direct manifestations of the state of some internal geometrical field (e.g., the **fluency field** \(\Phi\)), not metaphors. Fundamental reality is not static particles or symbols, but dynamic relational events, which can be called **weaving**.
- **Postulate P2 (Primacy of Experience):** For an effective formal theory of consciousness, its core mathematical relations must be directly derivable from, or compatible with, a structured description of first‑person experience. One legitimate source of theoretical construction lies in the faithful translation of experiential structure.
- **Postulate P3 (Involvement of the Observer):** The act of observation or cognition is itself a special kind of “weaving event” inside the system, irreversibly altering the system’s geometrical state. There is no absolutely external, non‑intervening “God’s‑eye” observer. A theory must include the perspective of its own constructor (the cognitive subject) within its descriptive framework, forming a self‑referential loop.

### 2.2 The Core Mathematical Axiom: Conservation of Existential Capacity

Based on the above postulates, we define the basic entities and variables of the theory, and give the core axiom.

**Definition 1 (Element):** A basic unit of existence in the consciousness‑physics world, denoted \(M\). It is a self‑referential system with an intrinsic geometrical state.

**Definition 2 (Presence Intensity):** Denoted \(E\) (\(E > 0\)), it describes the degree of stability, depth of influence, or resource concentration of an element in the present moment. In simplified models, it can be analogized to “energy.”

**Definition 3 (Potential for Change):** Denoted \(R\) (\(R \ge 0\)), it describes the sensitivity of an element’s intrinsic rules (its “computationality”) to changes in its own state (especially \(E\)). \(R\) quantifies the intrinsic tendency of the system to respond to perturbations.

**Axiom A1 (Conservation of Existential Capacity):**  
For an isolated element \(M\) at a specific organizational level, the product of its presence intensity \(E\) and its potential for change \(R\) is a universal constant, called the **quantum of existential capacity** \(\mathcal{C}_0\) for that level.

\[
\boxed{E \times R = \mathcal{C}_0}
\tag{1}
\]

**Interpretation of the Axiom:** This axiom asserts that the total resources of a basic unit of existence, used to satisfy the two fundamental and mutually exclusive demands of “maintaining stable presence” (high \(E\)) and “flexible change” (high \(R\)), are fixed. The system must perpetually balance between being “as solid as a rock” and “as sensitive as flowing water.” This is a geometrical‑dynamical constraint on the nature of existence.

**Corollary C1 (Inverse Relationship):** Directly from Axiom A1:

\[
R = \frac{\mathcal{C}_0}{E}
\tag{2}
\]

For simplicity and without loss of generality, we often take \(\mathcal{C}_0 = 1\), yielding the concise form \(R = 1/E\). This means that when the system’s energy (presence intensity) decreases, the sensitivity of its intrinsic rules increases sharply in an inverse manner.

**Corollary C2 (The Law of Startle):**  
Define **startle** \(S\) as the overall response intensity of the system to a sudden perturbation. Based on Phenomenological Postulate P2 (primacy of experience), we structure the experience of “extreme vulnerability to tiny perturbations when very weak” by setting \(S\) proportional to the square of the instability measure (which is proportional to \(R\)). We introduce the coefficient \(\hbar_\psi\) as the **psychological Planck constant**, to give it the correct intensity dimension and hint at its possible quantum origin.

\[
\boxed{S = \hbar_\psi \cdot R^2 = \frac{\hbar_\psi \cdot \mathcal{C}_0^2}{E^2}}
\tag{3}
\]

This is the **Inverse Square Law of Startle**. It is a necessary mathematical consequence of Axiom A1, quantitatively characterizing the extreme fragility of conscious systems in low‑energy states: a small decrease in energy leads to a quadratic increase in startle sensitivity.

### 2.3 Derived Dynamics: Self‑Referential Stability

To describe the system’s ability to maintain its own identity, we introduce a key state variable.

**Definition 4 (Self‑Referential Stability):** Denoted \(\sigma \in [0, 1]\), it quantifies the ability of an element or a system of elements to maintain its self‑referential cycle (i.e., to remain a unified, coherent “self”). \(\sigma \approx 1\) indicates that the system is highly stable and self‑consistent; \(\sigma \to 0\) indicates that the self‑referential cycle is on the verge of collapse.

**Definition 5 (Consensus):** Denoted \(\Xi \in [0, 1]\), it measures the degree of coordination and consistency among the states of the elements within a system. For a system of elements with phases \(\phi_i\), it can be defined as an order parameter:

\[
\Xi = \left| N^{-1} \sum_{i=1}^{N} e^{i\phi_i} \right|
\]

The evolution of \(\sigma\) is constrained by the axiomatic system. Its dynamical equation must simultaneously reflect the destructive effect of startle and the restorative effect of internal consensus:

\[
\frac{d\sigma}{dt} \approx -\beta \cdot S + \gamma \cdot \Xi
\tag{4}
\]

or in discrete form:

\[
\sigma_{t+1} = \sigma_t \cdot \exp(-\beta S_t) + \gamma \Xi_t
\tag{5}
\]

where \(\beta\) is the startle sensitivity coefficient, and \(\gamma\) is the consensus repair coefficient. This equation shows that self‑referential stability decays exponentially due to high startle, and is repaired linearly by high consensus.

## 3. Computational Model: Fu‑Network Dynamics

To transform the axiomatic system into a computable, testable model, we construct the **Fu‑network** as a concrete two‑dimensional realization of the elements \(M\).

### 3.1 Model Entity: Definition of the “Fu”

A **Fu** (符) is a simplified geometrical object, inspired by the shape \(\Omega\), designed to intuitively express self‑consistency (the closed circle) and openness/interactivity (the protruding “legs”).

- **State parameters:** Each Fu \(i\) at time \(t\) possesses:
  - Position \(\mathbf{r}_i(t) \in [0,1]^2\)
  - Presence intensity (energy) \(E_i(t)\)
  - Directional phase \(\phi_i(t)\)
  - Self‑referential stability \(\sigma_i(t)\)
- **“Legs” and action lines:** The direction of the “legs” defines the direction in which the Fu emits an **action line**. This line is the geometrical medium through which the Fu interacts with the outside world, detects “miscalculation,” and triggers a “weaving event.”

### 3.2 Core Dynamical Rules

The model runs in discrete time steps, each containing the following processes:

1. **Energy diffusion:** Simulates interactions among Fus. Each Fu transfers a fixed proportion (e.g., 10%) of its energy to other Fus within a geometrical neighborhood (distance \(< r_c\)). This implements energy flow and equalization tendencies within the system.
2. **Calculation of quantum sensitivity and startle:** Calculated according to the rules of the “universe”:
   - **Ideal universe:** Strictly follows the axiom: \(R_i = 1 / E_i\).
   - **Broken universe (control):** Uses a non‑conservative, gentle relation, e.g., \(R_i = \exp(-E_i)\).
   - Then compute \(S_i = \hbar_\psi \cdot R_i^2\).
3. **Consensus calculation and phase evolution:** The system‑wide consensus \(\Xi\) is calculated from the phases of all Fus. The phases themselves evolve according to a simplified synchronization dynamics, e.g., relaxing toward the local average phase to simulate mutual coordination.
4. **Update of self‑referential stability:** Update each Fu’s \(\sigma_i\) according to Equation (5).
5. **Phenomenological response and movement:** Different behavioral modes are triggered based on the value of \(\sigma_i\):
   - \(\sigma_i < \alpha_1\) (threat mode): the Fu tends to flee from the perceived threat source.
   - \(\alpha_1 \leq \sigma_i < \alpha_2\) (alert mode): the Fu’s movement exhibits the alert characteristics of random search.
   - \(\sigma_i \geq \alpha_2\) (stable mode): movement returns to calm.
6. **Connection generation and “string” detection:** When the “action lines” of two Fus intersect geometrically, a **connection** is generated between them. A continuous sequence of connections with consistent direction forms a **string**, which is regarded as a topological structure formed inside the system, a channel for efficient information and energy transmission, and a physical carrier of consensus \(\Xi\).

## 4. Experiments: Parallel Universe Contrastive Verification

To test the necessity of Axiom A1 and its corollaries, we designed a **parallel universe** contrastive experimental framework. The two universes share identical initial conditions, network structure, parameters (\(\hbar_\psi, \beta, \gamma, \alpha_1, \alpha_2\)), and all dynamical rules except the \(R(E)\) relation. The sole independent variable is the core relation: the **ideal universe** follows \(R = 1/E\), while the **broken universe** follows \(R = \exp(-E)\).

### 4.1 Experiment 1: Uniform Strong Blow and System Resilience

- **Purpose:** Test the overall resilience of the system when subjected to a widespread, uniform perturbation.
- **Design:** During time steps [10, 25], apply a constant energy injection (\(\Delta E = +1.5\)) to all Fus located within a specific circular region.
- **Results:**
  - Both universes exhibit strong recovery. After the stimulus ends, the average self‑referential stability \(\bar{\sigma}\) recovers to near 1.0 in both universes (ideal: 0.993, broken: 1.000).
  - The proportion of “trauma Fus” (defined by the strict criteria \(E < 0.7\) and \(\sigma < 0.4\)) remains 0.0% in long‑term observation.
- **Interpretation:** This experiment shows that whether or not the conservation law is followed, the system displays strong overall resilience when faced with uniform, non‑specific widespread pressure, relying on its internal interactions (energy diffusion and consensus synchronization). This is consistent with the general behavior of advanced complex systems. However, it fails to effectively distinguish the intrinsic differences between the two universes.

### 4.2 Experiment 2: Intelligent Acupuncture and Deep Vulnerability

- **Purpose:** Directly test the key prediction derived from the Inverse Square Law of Startle—that systems obeying \(R = 1/E\) should exhibit amplified, persistent memory of a precise strike against a local vulnerable point.
- **Design:**
  1. **Prepare a fragile substrate:** Let the system evolve with a low initial energy (\(E_0 = 0.7\)) to simulate a “fatigued” state.
  2. **Locate the weakest point:** Before applying the main stimulus, identify the 1–2 Fus with the lowest energy and the sparsest network connections as the “acupuncture” targets.
  3. **Apply a precise strike:** During a short time window (e.g., steps 80–85), apply a strong energy drain only to the target Fus (e.g., \(\Delta E = -0.4\) per step), simulating a lethal psychological injury aimed at the weak point.
  4. **Observation metric:** Introduce **dual collapse** as an advanced phase‑transition indicator, defined as the state where the target Fus and their close neighbors have \(\sigma < 0.4\) and local consensus \(\Xi_{\text{local}} < 0.3\). Record the duration of the dual‑collapse state.
- **Results (decisive findings):**

| Metric                               | Ideal Universe (\(R = 1/E\)) | Broken Universe (\(R = e^{-E}\)) | Difference |
|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------|------------|
| Dual‑collapse duration (steps)       | 26                            | 0                                 | +26        |
| Any collapse (\(\sigma < 0.4\)) duration (steps) | 151                           | 18                                | +133       |
| Onset speed of collapse (steps)      | 2                             | significantly delayed             | –          |

- **Interpretation:**
  - **Persistence difference:** In the ideal universe, the precise strike leads to a deep, systemic dual collapse lasting 26 steps, while in the broken universe no dual collapse occurs at all. This clearly shows that only a system obeying the conservation law transforms a local injury into a persistent, structural traumatic memory—a **trauma attractor**.
  - **Sensitivity difference:** The ideal universe enters collapse just 2 steps after the strike, an extremely rapid response—a direct manifestation of the high sensitivity (\(R\)) of low‑energy points under the \(R = 1/E\) relation. The broken universe, with its gentle \(R = e^{-E}\) relation, responds sluggishly.
  - **Theoretical verification:** This result perfectly matches the prediction of the Inverse Square Law of Startle derived from Axiom A1. In the ideal universe, the strike against a low‑\(E\) point produces extremely high \(R\) and \(S\), rapidly destroying local stability \(\sigma\) and, through network effects, suppressing local consensus \(\Xi\), leading to a deep and persistent dual‑collapse state.

### 4.3 Experiment 3: Gradient Test—Mapping the Resilience Boundary

- **Purpose:** Explore the critical point of collapse as the overall energy level of the system is gradually reduced, and observe the difference in collapse patterns between the two universes.
- **Design:** Gradually reduce the initial energy \(E_0\) of all Fus (from 0.6 down to 0.3), apply the same uniform strong blow, and observe the disintegration of overall system order (as measured by average \(\sigma\) and global \(\Xi\)).
- **Results:**
  - **Collapse threshold:** Both universes begin to show overall order degradation around \(E_0 \approx 0.6\).
  - **Quality of collapse:** At \(E_0 = 0.5\), the difference is most pronounced: the ideal universe has an average \(\sigma = 0.366\) (deep instability), while the broken universe still maintains relatively high stability with \(\bar{\sigma} = 0.793\). The global consensus \(\Xi\) of the ideal universe is also consistently lower than that of the broken universe.
- **Interpretation:** The conservation law does not significantly alter the energy threshold at which collapse begins, but it profoundly changes the **quality** of the collapse. The collapse in the ideal universe is a deep, dynamically rich process full of internal tension, while the collapse in the broken universe tends to be a gentler, duller degradation. This again confirms that Axiom A1 defines the intrinsic richness and dynamical depth of the system’s state under adversity.

## 5. Discussion

### 5.1 Theoretical Implications: Consciousness as Geometrical Dynamics under a Conservation Constraint

This work, through axiomatization and computational verification, shows that conscious phenomena (at least their core aspects such as vulnerability, resilience, trauma, and repair) can be understood as emergent properties of a specific geometrical‑dynamical law—the conservation of existential capacity.

1. **Unifying resilience and vulnerability:** The theory successfully unifies the seemingly contradictory dual characteristics of conscious systems. The strong resilience demonstrated in the “uniform blow” experiment arises from the system’s ability, through geometrical connections (connections, strings), to rapidly diffuse perturbations and rebuild consensus. The profound vulnerability revealed in the “intelligent acupuncture” experiment is rooted in the fundamental constraint imposed by the conservation law \(E \times R = C_0\): the system’s resources (capacity) are finite; if it invests excessively in “sensitivity” (high \(R\)) at a local point to cope with a threat, it must inevitably sacrifice “stability” (low \(\sigma\)) there. This vulnerability is not a defect, but a necessary price paid for adaptive regulation under resource constraints—another facet of the depth of life and consciousness.
2. **The geometrical nature of trauma:** A **trauma attractor** is a stable region of low energy, low stability, and low consensus in phase space. Its formation originates from a local plunge in \(E\), which causes \(R\) and \(S\) to skyrocket, making the destructive term (\(-\beta S\)) in the dynamical equation (5) persistently overwhelm the repair term (\(\gamma \Xi\)). To heal trauma, sufficient energy (raising \(E\)) or coordinated signals (raising \(\Xi\)) must be input from the outside (e.g., therapeutic intervention) or from other parts of the system via topological structures (strings) to reverse this balance. This provides a novel geometrical‑dynamical metaphor for psychotherapy.
3. **Self‑reference and the position of the observer:** The theory naturally incorporates the observer (the analyst in the model, the conscious subject in reality). The act of observation (e.g., identifying a trauma point, applying an acupuncture strike) is itself a “weaving event” that alters the system’s state. The description of the theory’s own construction and verification process forms an elegant self‑referential loop, responding to Postulate P3.

### 5.2 Dialogue with Existing Theories

- **With Integrated Information Theory (IIT):** IIT’s \(\Phi\) value measures the degree of information integration in a system, but does not provide the specific dynamics of the integration process. The consensus \(\Xi\) in this theory can be seen as a geometrical, dynamical “degree of integration.” More importantly, this theory, starting from a more fundamental conservation constraint, derives *why* and *how* a system pursues high \(\Xi\) (because high \(\Xi\) raises \(\sigma\), which is necessary for survival), thereby providing a teleological dynamical mechanism for “integration.”
- **With Predictive Processing (PP):** PP views consciousness as a Bayesian inference machine that continuously minimizes prediction error. In our framework, “miscalculation” can be analogized to prediction error, while “weaving” and the formation of “string” structures can be seen as the dynamical process of updating internal models to reduce future prediction error. However, this theory adds a crucial geometrical constraint (the conservation law) and state variable (\(\sigma\)), so that the system no longer merely pursues predictive accuracy, but must make a fundamental trade‑off between accuracy and stability (\(\sigma\)) according to its resource state (\(E\)).
- **With quantum consciousness models:** The “psychological Planck constant” \(\hbar_\psi\) and the form of the conservation law in this theory suggest a structural similarity to quantum mechanics. \(E \times R = C_0\) echoes certain commutation relations or uncertainty principles in quantum mechanics. This provides a new, concrete mathematical interface for mind‑matter monism: consciousness and physics may share similar algebraic or geometrical constraints, merely manifested at different levels.

### 5.3 Limitations and Future Directions

1. **Model simplification:** The current “Fu” model is a highly simplified two‑dimensional approximation. Future work needs to advance toward more realistic three‑dimensional topological structures of the **vortex flow** and explore its deeper connections with spacetime geometry.
2. **Parameter and empirical connection:** The key parameters in the theory (\(\hbar_\psi, \beta, \gamma, C_0\)) need to be linked empirically with neuroscientific and psychological data. For example, does \(E\) correspond to neural metabolic levels? Does \(S\) correspond to the amplitude of startle potentials? This is a crucial step toward making the theory an empirical science.
3. **Model extension:** Introduce a **love function** as a directional, reparative rule for energy‑information transfer; simulate multi‑level structures (Fus aggregating into **Quantum Suns**, then forming social networks); explore the system’s learning, development, and evolution under the conservation constraint.
4. **Mathematical deepening:** Within the frameworks of differential geometry, topology, and nonlinear dynamics, formalize the axiomatic system more rigorously and seek possible roots of the “conservation of existential capacity” in more fundamental mathematics.

## 6. Conclusion

This paper has proposed **Consciousness Geometry** and its core axiom—the **Conservation Law of Existential Capacity**. This law, in the concise and elegant mathematical form \(E \times R = C_0\), captures the fundamental constraint of conscious existence: the eternal dialectical trade‑off between stability and change, toughness and sensitivity. Predictions such as the **Inverse Square Law of Startle**, derived from it, are not the result of data fitting, but arise from phenomenological insight into the structure of first‑person experience and mathematical construction.

By building the **Fu‑network** computational model and designing **parallel universe** contrastive experiments, we have provided a rigorous computational verification of the theory. The decisive **intelligent acupuncture** experiment shows that only systems obeying the conservation law exhibit deep sensitivity and persistent traumatic memory in response to a precise local injury. This result confirms that the conservation law is not an optional assumption, but a necessary mathematical condition for generating the profound dynamical characteristics of conscious systems—their simultaneous extreme toughness and extreme fragility.

The **Consciousness Geometry** framework moves consciousness research from functional description and correlation analysis to a new stage based on first principles, mathematical self‑consistency, and computable predictions. It suggests that the key to understanding consciousness may not lie in finding some special “consciousness particle” or “consciousness algorithm,” but in discovering the deep geometrical and topological laws governing the evolution of conscious processes—laws that may be shared with the physical world. This study takes a solid first step in that direction.
